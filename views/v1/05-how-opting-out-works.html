If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS.

The opt-out process

You set an opt-out preference

Use our online service to opt-out of using your data beyond your individual care.

Your preference is processed

Our automated IT systems will process this information quickly and securely.

We make a record of your preference

This specifies the date your opt-out was received or if it has been withdrawn. So we know when we are able to use data and when we must apply an opt-out preference.

We update our full list of current preferences

It can take up to 21 days before your preference can be applied to future data releases.

Where opt-outs do not apply

There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply. In these circumstances, your personally identifiable data will still be used.

  • Where the information is used for purposes relating to your individual care
  • Where the personal confidential information does not contain your NHS number
  • If you have given consent for your data to be used for a specific reason, like a medical research study
  • Where data is anonymised which means you cannot be identified from the information
  • Where there is a legal requirement to provide information, such as an order of court
  • Where there is an overriding public interest in the disclosure of your data
  • Where information is used to support the management of communicable diseases and other risks to public health, like meningitis
  • Where patient registration information is supplied to the Office for National Statistics
