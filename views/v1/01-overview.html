Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These records contain your patient health and care information. They also contain personally identifiable data, like your name, postcode and date of birth.

Using your data

The NHS may use your personally identifiable data in two different ways.

Your individual care

Health and care professionals access your data to see your important health and care information. This may include:

  • current medical conditions
  • past procedures
  • dietary or allergy requirements

Research and planning

Your data is also used to improve the health of future generations. The NHS uses data to:

  • plan essential facilities like heart units
  • understand the need for specialist care wards
  • research potential cures for life-threatening diseases
  • improve treatment for serious illnesses

Where you have a choice

You can choose how your patient health information is used. If you don't agree with using your patient data for research and planning, you can opt out of this happening. The decision you make will not affect your individual care.
