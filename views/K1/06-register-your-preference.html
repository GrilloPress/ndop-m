You can use our online service to request that your personally identifiable data is not used or shared beyond your own individual care.

If you decide to opt-out, this will be respected by NHS Digital that collects, processes and releases health and care data on a national basis. It will also be respected by all other organisations that use health and social care information by March 2020.

Your decision will not affect your own individual care in any way. You can also change your mind and set a different preference in the future, if you wish to do so.

Before you start

You must have an email address or phone number registered with an NHS service to continue online. Other ways to set your preference.

To continue you will need:

  • access to your email or mobile phone
  • your NHS number
Where can I find my NHS number?

An NHS number is a 10 digit number, like 485 777 3456.

You can find your NHS number on any document sent to you by the NHS. This may include:

  • prescriptions
  • test results
  • hospital referral letters
  • appointment letters
  • your NHS medical card

Ask your GP for help if you can't find your NHS number
